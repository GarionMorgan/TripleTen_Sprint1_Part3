In honor of Joan Feynman: Women in astrophysics

Mon, January 12

Triple Peaks Library Conference Hall & Online

Reader picks

That our readers love

  • The Stand

    Stephen King's The Stand is a sweeping post-apocalyptic epic that begins with the accidental release of a weaponized superflu—nicknamed “Captain Trips”—from a U.S. military lab. The virus wipes out over 99% of the global population, leaving scattered survivors to navigate a shattered world. As these individuals begin to regroup, they’re drawn by prophetic dreams to two opposing leaders: the saintly Mother Abagail in Boulder, Colorado, and the malevolent Randall Flagg in Las Vegas. What follows is a battle between good and evil, as humanity’s remnants must choose between rebuilding a just society or succumbing to chaos and tyranny. Through richly drawn characters and moral dilemmas, King explores themes of faith, free will, and the enduring struggle between light and darkness.

    Stephen King

  • American Gods

    American Gods by Neil Gaiman follows Shadow Moon, a recently released convict who becomes the bodyguard of the mysterious Mr. Wednesday—actually the Norse god Odin. As they travel across America, Shadow is drawn into a hidden war between old gods fading from memory and new gods born from media and technology. Blending mythology with modern life, the novel explores belief, identity, and the soul of America through a surreal and haunting journey.

    Neil Gaiman

  • Dune

    Dune is a sweeping science fiction epic set on the desert planet Arrakis, the universe’s only source of the valuable spice melange. When young Paul Atreides’s noble family is betrayed, he must survive in the harsh desert, embrace his destiny among the native Fremen, and rise as a messianic figure in a battle for control of the planet and its future. The novel explores themes of ecology, religion, power, and prophecy in a richly imagined world.

    Frank Herbert

  • Lord of the Rings

    The Lord of the Rings follows the perilous journey of Frodo Baggins, a humble hobbit tasked with destroying the One Ring—a powerful artifact forged by the dark lord Sauron to control Middle-earth. Joined by a fellowship of allies, Frodo must navigate treacherous lands, resist the Ring’s corrupting influence, and confront overwhelming evil to save his world. The trilogy explores themes of friendship, sacrifice, courage, and the enduring struggle between light and darkness.

    J.R.R. Tolkien

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and the Triple Peaks Library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 monthly visitors. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and using our ample online resources.
